Kaidao ({font=tl/None/NotoSansCJKtc-Regular.otf}開道{/font})

In Mazu culture, Kaidao (lit. "clear the path") is a rite in the Holy Pilgrimage.

During the Kaidao rite, guardian tigers lead the way and expel demons along the route for all the other gods in the pilgrimage.
　　
The Holy Pilgrimage is a ceremony where worshippers bring large god statues on a grand tour. People believe that gods can inspect their domain and see their people during the tour.
